01 · The Mark

The Schedule Grid

The RxShift mark is a 4-column × 3-row grid of rounded schedule blocks. One column — always the third — is highlighted in Shift Amber. The mark is 100% geometric vector: no typeface, no text-to-outline requirement for the icon itself. It scales from a 16px favicon to a billboard without modification.

What the Grid Means

The mark is readable as a schedule at a glance — four columns, each a day or shift window; three rows, each a staffing block or time period. The amber column is the active shift: the one being staffed, the one that needs to be compliant right now. A pharmacist reads it immediately. A tech buyer reads it as data. Neither has to be told what it is.

Column 1

Past or far-future. Inactive, receding. Light slate.

Column 2

Upcoming. Present in the window but not current. Mid slate.

Column 3 — Active

The live shift. Needs coverage. Must be compliant. Amber.

Column 4

Future. Not yet in scope. Light slate, matching col 1.

The Wordmark

The wordmark is Rx · Shift: "Rx" in Space Grotesk 800, an amber middle dot separator, and "Shift" in Space Grotesk 500. The amber dot is not decorative — it mirrors pharmacy Rx notation, bridges the two halves of the name, and carries the brand color without requiring the full mark. When space permits, "Rx · Shift" is always paired with the grid mark. The wordmark never appears alone without the mark except in plain-text contexts (email body, legal docs).

Tagline

The official tagline is Compliance-ready pharmacy scheduling. Sentence case, no period in display contexts. It appears at first brand mention in marketing materials and always below the full stacked lockup when space allows. It is not part of the mark.

03 · Typography

Type System

Two typefaces. Space Grotesk carries all brand personality — wordmark, headlines, navigation, UI labels, buttons. Inter handles body copy and data-dense interfaces where readability at length matters more than character.

Space Grotesk — Brand Typeface

Geometric humanist sans-serif with distinctive angled terminals and a slightly idiosyncratic construction (the angled G aperture, the unique R shoulder, the rotated comma-period). Reads as precise without being cold. Available free via Google Fonts: weights 500 and 800 cover all brand needs.

04 · Voice & Tone

How We Sound

RxShift speaks like a pharmacist who's also good at software — knowledgeable without being clinical, direct without being terse. We never over-explain regulation. We describe exactly what the product does in plain language and trust the buyer to recognize the value.

The voice in three words: Confident. Precise. Warm.

Headlines

✗ Don't

Revolutionize your pharmacy scheduling with AI-powered compliance tools

Vague. "Revolutionize" and "AI-powered" are noise in every SaaS inbox.
✓ Do

Your schedule knows the ratios. So you don't have to.

Specific benefit. Buyer-side language. No filler.
✗ Don't

A compliance-ready workforce management platform for modern pharmacies

Generic SaaS positioning. Could describe twenty other tools.
✓ Do

Every shift documented. Every ratio covered. Before the inspector arrives.

Specific. Evocative. Names the fear and resolves it in two lines.

Email Subject Lines

✗ Don't

Unlock the power of automated scheduling today

Marketing speak. Vague urgency. No recipient-specific hook.
✓ Do

How Southwest Medical stopped tracking ratios by hand

Specific. Story-driven. The reader immediately sees themselves in it.

UI Copy — Button CTAs

✗ Don't

Submit / Proceed / Learn More

Non-specific. Doesn't say what the click actually does.
✓ Do

Generate Schedule · Export Compliance Log · Set Up Ratios

Active verbs. Names the exact action. Pharmacy-native vocabulary.

Status & Compliance Messages (Product UI)

✗ Don't

Cold. Accusatory. Leaves the user with nowhere to go.

Specific time window. Exact fix. No alarm, just direction.
05 · Usage Rules

Logo Usage

The Mark Is Pure Geometry

The schedule grid mark contains no typeface — it is 12 rounded rectangles arranged in a 4×3 grid. There is no text to outline, no font licensing concern for the icon itself. The mark can be exported as SVG directly from any vector editor and used in any context without modification. Only the wordmark "Rx · Shift" requires Space Grotesk to be present or outlined for print production.

Clearspace

Minimum clearspace on all sides of the mark: equal to the height of one grid cell. At the standard 22px cell size, that's 22px on each side. For the horizontal lockup, apply clearspace to the full bounding box of mark + wordmark together. Never crowd the mark against edges, type, or dividers — it needs air to read as a schedule rather than as decoration.

Minimum Sizes

Horizontal lockup: minimum 160px wide (screen), 42mm wide (print).
Stacked lockup: minimum 100px wide.
Mark only: minimum 20×15px. Below this the individual cells merge visually — use a simplified 2×2 version or a solid amber square as a favicon placeholder.
Wordmark alone (in plain-text contexts): set in Space Grotesk 800/500 or note "RxShift" in standard weight if fonts unavailable. Never stretch or condense.

Do

  • Use the light-background mark (navy/slate/amber cells) on white, Cloud, or any light neutral
  • Use the dark-background mark (dark navy cells + amber) on Navy Anchor or very dark surfaces
  • Scale the mark proportionally — never distort aspect ratio
  • Keep column 3 amber in all contexts; it is the defining element of the mark
  • Pair the mark with the wordmark "Rx · Shift" whenever space allows
  • Use the tagline "Compliance-ready pharmacy scheduling" at first brand mention in marketing
  • Write the brand name as RxShift — capital R, lowercase x, capital S, one word, always

Don't

  • Don't change column 3 from amber to any other color — the schedule metaphor breaks
  • Don't add a background badge or container shape to the mark; it stands alone
  • Don't place the mark on busy photography or patterned backgrounds
  • Don't use Deficiency Red (#C0392B) in any marketing context — status UI only
  • Don't use "RXShift", "rx shift", "Rxshift", or any other casing variant
  • Don't apply drop shadows, gradients, or glow effects to the mark
  • Don't pair RxShift with generic pharmacy imagery (pills, mortar, caduceus) — it contradicts the brand's positioning

Single-Color Use

For single-color contexts (legal headers, embossing, fax cover pages): render all grid cells in solid black (#000000) at equal weight. Distinguish the active column by making it solid black while inactive columns use 30% opacity black — or reverse it: inactive cells outline-only, active cell solid. For reversed single-color: all cells white on black. The grid structure remains the carrier even when color is removed.
